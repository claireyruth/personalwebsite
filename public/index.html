About

I am Claire Ruth McNellan.

I’m a doctoral student and researcher interested in applying epidemiological methods to the evaluation of child protection programs, policies, and systems. I am interested in front-end services to reduce risk for vulnerable families, equity concerns about surveillance and data tech, maternal/child health, and reimagining the child welfare system in the United States. I’ve helped bring over 15 quantitative analyses to publication.

I’ve worked with large social and health data sets at the Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME), the Public Health Foundation of India, the National Court Appointed Special Advocates (CASA)/Guardians ad Litem (GAL) Association for Children, the Global Center for Integrated Health of Women, Adolescents, and Children (Global WACh), and the Children’s Data Network. In 2017, I earned a fully-funded MPH in Global Health: Health Metrics and Evaluation from the University of Washington. I am a graduate of Colorado College, where I graduated with distinction in Neuroscience and designed and self-directed a Bioethics minor. In August of 2020, I began a Ph.D. program in Social Work at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.

My side hustles include Irish dancing, writing on my blog, and scavenging thrift stores in search of vintage kitchenware.
